This place has the most delicious kim chi I've ever had probably. Their brown rice is good (although it's actually purple-ish) and the Kalbi Jim here is really good. The seafood pancake was also pretty extraordinary, as it was crispy without fault. However, this place does almost require a group of four or more to get a minimal number of variety of dishes. Look for the huge number of Koreans crowding outside.
