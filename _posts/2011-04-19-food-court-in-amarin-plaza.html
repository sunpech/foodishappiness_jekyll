--- layout: post title: Food court in Amarin Plaza date: '2011-04-19T19:00:00.000-07:00' author: Steven Suwatanapongched tags: - Bangkok - Thai modified_time: '2011-04-19T19:00:01.342-07:00' thumbnail: https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/-d1elAGFRRWY/TWLdY05-9CI/AAAAAAAAgYs/kOz3U2snudY/s600/IMG_20110120_132112.jpg blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-632104503620322058.post-5760241732806256984 blogger_orig_url: http://www.foodishappiness.com/2011/04/food-court-in-amarin-plaza.html --- The food courts in Bangkok are generally a good place to try a lot of street-like vendor food, without actually going out on the street. I like it because:

  1. It collects a lot of different foods in one place (convenient)
  2. It's indoors
  3. It has plenty of seats
  4. It's air-conditioned
  5. It's cleaner than street food
  6. It's super cheap still
You can expect to spend less than 100 baht per person on a plate + a drink here, which at the current rate is about $3.28 US. I was super starving and ate 2 plates and 2 drinks here, and spent about 120 to 160 baht I think.
